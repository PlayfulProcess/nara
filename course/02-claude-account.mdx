# A Claude Account

Everything technical in this course — reading files, writing files, creating grammars, finding public-domain images, updating your repo — is done by **Claude**, an AI assistant you talk to in plain language at [claude.ai](https://claude.ai). You describe what you want; it does the file work.

This is the honest reason a non-coder can do this at all. You are not learning to code. You are learning to have a working conversation — to say what you mean, look at what came back, and say what to change.

## Set it up

1. Go to [claude.ai](https://claude.ai) and create an account.
2. Subscribe to a paid plan — about **$20/month** at the time of writing. The free tier is fine for chatting, but this course leans on **connectors** (chapter 3), longer conversations, and steady daily use, which is what the paid plan is for.
3. That is it. No installation. The phone browser or the phone app both work.

## Why the paid plan and not free

- Connectors — the two plugs in the next chapter that let Claude reach your repo and recursive.eco — need a paid account.
- You will have long, multi-step conversations ("research this, then build that, then fix the third item"). Free-tier limits interrupt those mid-thought.
- $20/month is the entire technical budget of this method. There is nothing else to buy.

## What Claude is, in this course

A pair of hands, not an oracle. It will happily make things up if you let it, so the working culture here is: ask it to **cite sources**, ask it to **show you what it did**, and check the result yourself in the app. You stay the author; it stays the assistant. The next chapters are built around that habit.

# What You Are Building

Before any account or setup, here is the whole shape of the thing. There are only three ideas.

## 1. A grammar

A **grammar** is a symbolic system: a deck of cards, a book of hexagrams, a set of signs, a collection of stories. Anything made of items that carry meaning. Tarot decks are grammars. The I Ching is a grammar. The twelve tropical signs are a grammar.

You are going to make one. Not by coding — by talking.

## 2. A repo

A **repo** (repository) is a free folder on GitHub that holds your project's files: your research notes, your journal, your data. It is the open home of your grammar — the part anyone can read, copy, and build on. It is also the project's memory: every conversation you have ends by writing what happened into the repo, so the next conversation can pick it up.

## 3. recursive.eco

[recursive.eco](https://flow.recursive.eco) is where your grammar lives **interactively** — where you and other people can read it, draw from it, journal with it. The repo is the open home; recursive.eco is the living room.

## The worked example: the Trika family

This course is not hypothetical. The project you are reading it inside — **Nara**, a study of the tropical zodiac as the human-subjective sky — was built exactly this way, and so was its sibling:

- **[kali-paradevi](https://github.com/PlayfulProcess/kali-paradevi)** — the objective deep sky: black holes and galaxies as paired reflective cards. Its first two grammars were built entirely from a phone, in conversation.
- **nara** (this repo) — the subjective sky: seasons, equinoxes, the year as lived from Earth.
- Together they form a family called **Trika**.

Everything you will do, these projects already did. The path is walked.

## What you do NOT need

No laptop (a phone works). No coding. No command line. No prior knowledge of astrology, astronomy, or anything else — curiosity about your own subject is the whole prerequisite.

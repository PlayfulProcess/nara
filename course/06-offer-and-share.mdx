# Offer and Share

Your grammar exists, your repo remembers, and so far only you can see any of it. This last chapter is the small set of moves that turn a private study into an offered one.

## Publish the grammar

In a chat with the connectors:

> "Make my 'Eight Trigrams' grammar public."

Claude flips the visibility through the recursive.eco connector. The grammar now appears in the public **Library**, and its link works for anyone — no account needed to read it. (You can flip it back to private the same way, anytime.)

Before you do: read it once more as a stranger. Published means someone you will never meet may sit with your words. The humility-charter test is a good one — does anything on the cards claim more than you actually know? If a line says "you are" or "you will," soften it into a question.

## Offer it to a channel

Channels are curated collections on recursive.eco — a kids channel, a tarot channel, someday perhaps one for your subject. Offering submits your grammar for a channel's curator to review.

This one currently happens **in the app, not in chat** (there is no connector tool for it yet — a known gap):

1. Open [flow.recursive.eco](https://flow.recursive.eco) on your phone and sign in.
2. Find your grammar and use the **Offer** button to submit it to a channel that fits.
3. The channel's curator reviews it. Pending, approved, or not — either way your grammar stays yours and stays in the Library.

Your account also has a **Personal Channel** — your own public page, which your starred grammars populate automatically. That is your altar, in the app's older vocabulary: the small collection you tend and point people to.

## Invite the first other person

The real completion of zero-to-one is the second person. Send one human two links:

- the grammar's live link — "read this, tell me where it is wrong";
- the repo link — "and this is how it was made."

If they want to change something, chapter 5 already built the doors: propose in the app, or a pull request on GitHub. You review, you merge. The moment you merge a stranger's improvement, your study project has become a small commons — which is the actual destination this course was quietly walking toward.

## Where you are now

Zero to one, done: an open repo with memory, a living grammar in the app, a published offering, and a working loop you can run from a phone for the rest of the project's life. Everything past this — casting shapes, channels of your own, courses like this one — is the same loop, repeated with patience.

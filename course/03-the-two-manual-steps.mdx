# The Two Manual Steps

This chapter is the entire technical setup of the method. Two connectors, plugged in once. After this, every remaining chapter is just talking.

A **connector** gives Claude hands into another service. You will plug in two: one into recursive.eco (so it can build grammars in the live app) and one into GitHub (so it can read and write your repo).

## Before you start

- You need a **recursive.eco account**: go to [flow.recursive.eco](https://flow.recursive.eco) and sign in once (email sign-in; free, comes with a little AI credit). The first connector will ask you to log in with this account.
- You need your **GitHub account** from chapter 1.
- Do this on the claude.ai **website** (phone browser is fine).

## Step A — the recursive.eco connector

1. Open [claude.ai/settings/connectors](https://claude.ai/settings/connectors).
2. Tap **Add custom connector**.
3. Where it asks for the URL, paste exactly:

   ```
   https://flow.recursive.eco/api/mcp
   ```

4. Tap **Add**, then **Connect**. A recursive.eco sign-in opens — sign in with **your recursive.eco account** and authorize.
5. You should see the connector listed as connected.

What this gives Claude: the grammar toolkit — creating grammars and items, searching Wikipedia and Wikimedia Commons for public-domain images, generating images, publishing. You never call these tools yourself; you just ask for things and Claude picks them.

**If it stalls:** the sign-in page usually means you were not yet logged in to recursive.eco — log in at [flow.recursive.eco](https://flow.recursive.eco) first, then retry **Connect**.

## Step B — the GitHub connector

1. On the same [connectors page](https://claude.ai/settings/connectors), find **GitHub** in the list of built-in connectors (no URL needed — it is first-party).
2. Tap **Connect** and authorize with your GitHub account. When GitHub asks which repositories, grant access to your project repo (or all your repos, your choice).

What this gives Claude: reading and writing files in your repo — your notes, your journal, your data. This is how the project remembers.

## One habit that saves confusion

Connectors are picked up **when a chat starts**. If you connect something mid-conversation and Claude cannot see it, do not fight it — just **start a new chat**. New chat, fresh hands.

---

**Do it now:** connect both, then start a NEW chat and type:

> "Can you see my recursive.eco tools and my GitHub repo? List my grammars and the files in my repo."

If it lists both (an empty grammar list is fine — you have not made one yet), the setup is finished forever. Everything from here is conversation.
